Who can vote?

All registered St. Lawrence County voters are eligible to vote in the General Election, regardless of party enrollment.

Qualifications to register

  • Must be a United States citizen
  • Must be 18 years old (you may pre-register at 16 or 17 but cannot vote until you are 18)
  • Must resident of this state and the county, city or village for at least 30 days before the election
  • Must not be in prison or on parole for a felony conviction (unless parolee pardoned or restored rights of citizenship)
  • Must not be adjudged mentally incompetent by a court
  • You must not be claiming the right to vote elsewhere

Any duly registered voter is eligible to vote in a General Election, and in a Primary Election voters who are enrolled in the party that is holding the Primary are eligible.

How to register

Registering in person

Registration forms are available at most government offices and post offices, or you may register in person Monday through Friday at the St. Lawrence County Board of Elections Office, 80 State Hwy 310, Canton, New York, between 9am and 5pm. Our summer hours, between Memorial Day and Labor Day, are 8am to 4pm.

Requesting voter registration form over the phone

Call 315 379-2202 and a registration form will be sent to you in the mail to complete and return.

Register by mail or request registration application by e-mail

Print an application form here complete and return through the mail; or, e-mail your request for a registration form to be sent to you. Be sure to include your mailing address.

If you want to vote in an election, you must mail or deliver your completed registration form to the Board of Elections no later than 25 days before the election in which you want to vote. Your eligibility to vote will be based on the date you file this form, and the county board will notify you of such.

Registration application directions

  • Complete the entire application. Remember to check the box next to the political party in which you wish to enroll. If you do not wish to be affiliated with any party, check the appropriate box.
  • Sign and date your application in blue or black ink. This is required!
  • Mail the completed application. Send to: St. Lawrence County Board of Elections 80 State Highway 310, Canton, NY 13617
  • Download the voter registration form Here

Re-registering or changing your information

  • Name, address or party enrollment changes can be made by submitting a new registration form in any manner permitted for a registration application.
  • If you move, your notice of change of address must be received by the Board of Elections up to 20 days before the election in which you seek to vote.
  • If you have missed the 20 day deadline, you may contact the Board of Elections for your new polling site.

Early voting

Early voting will occur at the Canton Board of Elections office (at the Human Services Building, 80 State Highway 310, Second floor) in the two weeks before each upcoming Special, Primary or General Election

Absentee voting

You can still vote if you are unable to make it to your polling place on Election Day. Turn in your absentee ballot by mail or bring it to the Board of Elections office

When is it due?

An absentee ballot must be postmarked by the day before Election Day and must reach the Board of Elections no more than 7 days after the election to be counted

Absentee voting qualifications

  • Absent from St. Lawrence County on election day
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to illness or disability
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to duties related to primary care of one or more individuals who are ill or physically disabled
  • A patient in a Veteransâ€™ Administration Hospital
  • Detained in jail awaiting Grand Jury action or confined in prison after conviction for an offense other than a felony

Permenantly disabled absentee voting qualifications

  • Permanent Illness
  • Physical Disability
  • These voters will be put on a permanent list and receive a ballot for each election in which they are eligibile to vote

Download a permenantly disabled absentee ballot application here.

Military or federal absentee voting qualifications

  • Actively in the Military, eligible spouse, or dependent
  • Activated National Guard member on State orders
  • Additional information can be found by visiting the Federal Voting Assistance Program website

Download a military or federal absentee ballot application here.

Special federal absentee voting qualifications

  • For United States citizens living abroad whose last U.S. address was in St. Lawrence County
  • Applicants may only vote for Federal Offices, unless their application indicates an intent to return
  • For United States citizens who have never lived in the U.S

Download a special federal absentee ballot application here.

Federal write-in absentee ballot

  • For use by Military voters, wherever they are posted
  • For use by Special Federal voters living outside the United States
  • If you do not receive your military or federal absentee ballot in enough time to meet New York State's deadlines, use this ballot as a backup. If you send in this ballot and then receive your official ballot later, you should fill out and send in that official ballot as well. Your election office will count only one ballot.
  • New York State requires you to register and request an absentee ballot by completing a Federal Post Card Application form before filling out and submitting this backup form. This FPCA form is available above as well as by visiting the Federal Voting Assistance Program website
  • If you already registered and requested an absentee ballot, send in the Voter Information page and the Official Backup Ballot
  • Not for use by Regular Absentee Voters or Permanent Absentee Voters
